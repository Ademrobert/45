Hi! I’m Rachel, a Workshop Experience Designer and Facilitator based in the United States.

I have over 18 years experience across design thinking, brand and creative strategy, workshop creation, and facilitation—Wow! That made me feel old. Although, I like to think I’ve aged like a great whiskey. I’m a firm believer that play is the doorway to our wildest ideas, and as a result, I’ve built my own consulting company to help others bring that joy and energy into their workshops.

 

Coming from an environment where being creative was like pulling teeth, I’ve worked throughout my career to help others move past the thoughts that they are ‘just not creative.’ Community is also a huge part of my journey. I lead a Miro Community Group and help run the ButterMixers, a twice monthly session with the curious minds over at the Butter Community.

 

I look for inspiration everywhere and one of my favorite things to explore are books. I probably have way too many books on my reading list right now, so don’t ever hesitate to ask me for a suggestion on what to read next!

02

Designing Workshop Experiences — The Art of Playful Collaboration

Ready to learn how to unearth an abundance of new and unique ideas from your team? Want to tap into their creative potential and bring a sense of joy to the process?

 

If you're tired of boring old workshops and brainstorming sessions that leave you feeling drained and uninspired, it's time to add some sparkle to your collaborative sessions and turn those yawn-fest meetings into idea-generating, wonder-filled experiences!

 

Get ready to tap into your childlike mindset and intentionally design workshops that encourage curiosity and energy. This workshop will be a rollercoaster ride of creativity, laughter, and inspiration, and you don't want to miss it! Are you ready to go from yawn to yay and design some seriously playful sessions that ignite a creative spark in all of us?

 

What you’ll get out of this workshop:

 

  • Dig deeper into the ‘why’ of using play in collaboration and facilitation.
  • Pick up some creative (and fun!) techniques that get the group away from the blank page trap, which we affectionately call the “swan in the snowstorm.”
  • Learn other imaginative methods to get people absorbed in playful collaboration.
  • Actionable approaches you can start using right away with your team.
